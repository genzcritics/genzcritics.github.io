The Cabinet Decision Offers Clever Political Commentary

by Elizabeth Kim on August 28, 2016

In an age dominated by vague politics and messy wars, animated short The Cabinet Decision is refreshingly simple: Israel and Palestine are comparable to a pair of conjoined twins who fail to realize that they must work together to survive and instead, bring about their own destruction. The film cuts between shots of the narrator, a portly scientist sitting in a lab, and those of the fighting twins, who become irritated with each other while building sand castles in the desert. As they exchange blows and attempt to separate, they appear increasingly foolish; the fruitlessness and stupidity of their squabble becomes all too obvious to the viewer, but the twins continue their altercation. The narrator discusses the dilemma of the twins and proceeds to list the countless ramifications of a potential separation surgery. Though the goal of the surgery would be to allow at least one twin to survive, it is implied that any attempts to completely separate Israel from Palestine would result in the annihilation of both regions. Later on, a pair of separated conjoined bugs lying in a pool of blood dominates the frame, hammering home the point that Israel and Palestine are unlikely to realize the truth until it is too late.
